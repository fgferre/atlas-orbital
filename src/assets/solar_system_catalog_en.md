## Mercury

- Name: Mercury
- Type: Terrestrial Planet
- Mass: 3.301 × 10²³ kg
- Radius: 2,439.7 km
- Average distance from the Sun: 57,910,000 km
- Gravity: 3.7 m/s²
- Composition: Rock and iron
- Atmosphere: Very tenuous, composed of traces of sodium, potassium, oxygen, helium
- Day length (rotation): 58.6 Earth days
- Year length (revolution): 87.97 Earth days
- Curiosity: It is the closest planet to the Sun and has no moons.
- Fact: Its surface has extreme temperature variations, from -173°C to 427°C.
- Description: Mercury is small, dense, and has craters similar to those of the Moon, as well as a proportionally large metallic core.

## Venus

- Name: Venus
- Type: Terrestrial Planet
- Mass: 4.867 × 10²⁴ kg
- Radius: 6,051.8 km
- Average distance from the Sun: 108,200,000 km
- Gravity: 8.87 m/s²
- Composition: Silicate rocks
- Atmosphere: 96% carbon dioxide, clouds of sulfuric acid
- Day length: 243 Earth days (retrograde rotation)
- Year length: 224.7 Earth days
- Curiosity: Its atmosphere generates the strongest greenhouse effect in the solar system.
- Fact: Venus is the hottest planet, with surface temperatures above 460°C.
- Description: Similar in size to Earth, Venus shines intensely in the sky, but its environment is extremely inhospitable.

## Sun

- Name: Sun
- Type: Star
- Spectral class: G2V
- Mass: 1.989 × 10³⁰ kg
- Radius: 696,340 km
- Average distance from Earth: 149,600,000 km (1 AU)
- Surface gravity: 274 m/s²
- Composition: 73% hydrogen, 25% helium, 2% heavier elements
- Atmosphere: Photosphere, chromosphere, corona
- Day length (rotation): ~24.47 days (equator), ~34 days (poles)
- Year length: Not applicable (central point of the system)
- Curiosity: It accounts for approximately 99.86% of the total mass of the solar system.
- Facts:
  - Its light takes about 8 minutes and 20 seconds to reach Earth.
  - Solar cycle of 11 years, alternating between maxima and minima of activity.
  - Generates energy through nuclear fusion, converting hydrogen into helium.
- Description: The Sun is a middle-aged star, the primary source of energy and heat for the entire solar system. Its enormous gravitational influence keeps planets, asteroids, comets, and TNOs in orbit. Its atmospheric layers and phenomena such as solar flares affect space weather and influence technological systems on Earth.
